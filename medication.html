Medication Management

Cardiac and Respiratory Medications Guide

Heart Medications

Beta Blockers Medium Risk
Common Names:
Metoprolol, Atenolol, Propranolol
Purpose:
Reduce blood pressure, manage arrhythmias
Dosage:
25-200mg/day depending on condition
ACE Inhibitors Low Risk
Common Names:
Lisinopril, Enalapril, Ramipril
Purpose:
Treat hypertension and heart failure
Dosage:
5-40mg/day depending on condition
Diuretics Medium Risk
Common Names:
Furosemide, Hydrochlorothiazide
Purpose:
Reduce fluid retention and blood pressure
Dosage:
20-80mg/day depending on severity

Oxygen-Related Medications

Bronchodilators Low Risk
Common Names:
Albuterol, Salbutamol, Ipratropium
Purpose:
Improve airflow in lungs
Dosage:
1-2 puffs every 4-6 hours as needed
Corticosteroids Medium Risk
Common Names:
Prednisone, Budesonide, Fluticasone
Purpose:
Reduce lung inflammation
Dosage:
5-60mg/day depending on severity
Anticoagulants High Risk
Common Names:
Warfarin, Rivaroxaban, Apixaban
Purpose:
Prevent blood clots in lung conditions
Dosage:
Strictly weight-dependent, requires monitoring
Important: Medication should only be taken under medical supervision. Risk levels are general guidelines and may vary by individual patient circumstances.
